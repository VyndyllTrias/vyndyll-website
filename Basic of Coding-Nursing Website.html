NURSING: NEWBORN ASSESSMENT

APGAR SCORING

  • APGAR was created by Dr. Virginia Apgar in 1952.
  • Since then, doctors and nurses have been evaluating newborns one minute and five minutes after born using the APGAR rating system. Typically, it is administered twice, once at the moment of birth and once more five minutes afterward.
  • APGAR checks a baby's heart rate, muscle tone, and other signs that may require emergency care.

APGAR SCORE MEAN?


0-3 Are critically low, especially in term and late-preterm infants.
4-6 Are below normal, and indicate that the baby likely requires medical intervention.
7+ or Above are considered normal
